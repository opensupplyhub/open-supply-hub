Product Delivery Roadmap Meeting — How It Runs

What this meeting is

  • Recurring check-in on big cross-org projects. We surface dependencies, timeline risks, and blockers — we don't solve them in the room.
  • Goals: awareness across the org and triaging next actions.
  • The dashboard is the source of truth. It pulls live from Jira. Use your update time on what the dashboard doesn't show.

Team Participation

You're in the room to bring your domain lens. It's an opportunity to support the team. Project owners have blind spots outside their domain — pricing before a contract, legal review before a deadline, GTM needs, partner dynamics, dependencies they don't see. As each project comes up, listen for what touches your area and flag it. Don't wait to be asked.

What "at risk" means

A project moves to at risk when any of these are true:

  • A blocker is stopping the team from hitting the timeline
  • Allocated resources have moved or been pulled
  • Newly uncovered work means the effort will exceed its day allocation (e.g., 30 days instead of 20)
  • A defined success metric isn't trending toward the goal
  • An external dependency (partner, vendor, funder) is slipping
Workstream Owner Notes

Before the meeting (homework for workstream owners)

By end of day Monday before every meeting, update in Jira:

  • Epic-level: Status, Health Status, Product Execution Update (fresh entry each meeting), Start Date, Due Date
  • Ticket-level: Built-out backlog covering the full scope of work (placeholders are fine), ticket statuses current
  • Make sure your timeline bar reflects the real shape of the work — if it runs into Q3/Q4, put placeholder tickets there so the bar isn't misleadingly short

Workstream owner update — 1 minute, in this order

  1. What is this project? One sentence. Assume someone in the room has never heard of it.
  2. Where in the lifecycle? Ideating → Exec review → Refining → Prototyping → 2nd exec review → Productionizing. Name where you are.
  3. Status and why. On track, at risk, or off track — and the reason in 1–3 sentences. If you're flagging at risk, come with a question for the group — what do you need help thinking through? At risk isn't a fault, it's a call for support.
  4. The details that matter: scope changes since last check, upcoming dependencies (what you need from whom, by when), blockers, and a win or shoutout if you've got one.
  5. Cross-team collaborators. Name the point person from Programs (and any other team) currently staffed on this project. Flag if that person's availability or fit needs revisiting given where the work is right now.
  6. Upcoming asks. Any requests or support you anticipate needing from another team in the next two weeks — what it is, who it's for, and roughly how much time you expect it to take.
Facilitator Notes
  • Start with at-risk items first, then work down by priority
  • Hard caps: 1 min update, 2 min discussion per project
  • Box anything that needs deeper work — assign an action, name a follow-up container, move on
  • Notetaker captures actions and distributes after
